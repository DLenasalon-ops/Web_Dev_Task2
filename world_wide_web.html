World Wide Web

WWW illustration

Definition:

The World Wide Web (WWW) is an information system where documents and other web resources are identified by URLs, interlinked by hypertext links, and can be accessed via the Internet.

It was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 and has since become a fundamental part of modern life, enabling the sharing of information, communication, and commerce on a global scale.
